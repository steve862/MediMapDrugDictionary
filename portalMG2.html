-TUBERCULOSIS-

RIFAMPIN(Rifampicin)

Description:


is used together with other medicines to treat tuberculosis (TB) in many different parts of the body. It belongs to the class of medicines called antibiotics and works to kill or prevent the growth of bacteria.
Administration: Oral route
Dosage: This medicine is available in capsule form and only with doctor's prescription. It is very important that you take this medicine for the full time of treatment. If you have TB, you might have to take rifampin every day for several months. Take the capsule on an empty stomach, 1 hour before or 2 hours after a meal, with a full of glass water. If this medicine upsets your stomach, take it with food.

For oral dosage form (capsules):


-For the treatment of meningitis bacteria in the nose or throat:

-Adults—600 milligrams (mg) 2 times a day for 2 days.
-Children 1 month of age and older—Dose is based on body weight and must be determined by your doctor. The dose is 10 mg per kilogram (kg) of body weight every 12 hours for 2 days.
-Children younger than 1 month of age—Dose is based on body weight and must be determined by your doctor. The dose is 5 mg per kg of body weight every 12 hours for 2 days.


For treatment of tuberculosis:

-Adults—Dose is based on body weight and must be determined by your doctor. The dose is 10 milligrams (mg) per kilogram (kg) of body weight once a day. Your doctor may increase your dose as needed. However, the dose is usually not more than 600 mg per day.
-hildren—Dose is based on body weight and must be determined by your doctor. The dose is 10 to 20 mg per kg of body weight once a day. Your doctor may increase your dose as needed. However, the dose is usually not more than 600 mg per day.




If you miss a dose of this medicine, take it as soon as possible. However, if it is almost time for your next dose, skip the missed dose and go back to your regular dosing schedule. Do not double doses. Side Effects:
