-CORONARY HEART DISEASE-

ATORVASTATIN

Description:

Atorvastatin is in a class of medications called HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors (statins). It is used together with diet, weight loss, and exercise to reduce the risk of heart attack and stroke.

Function:

It slows the production of cholesterol in the body to decrease the amount of cholesterol that may build up on the walls of the arteries and block blood flow to the heart, brain, and other parts of the body.

Dosage (for adults 18-64 years):


Typical starting dosage: 10-20 mg taken once per day.
Typical maintenance dosage: 10-80 mg taken once per day.

Dosage (For children):


-Children 10 years of age and older with heterozygous familial hypercholesterolemia (HeFH)—starting dose: 10 mg once a day. Doctor may increase dosage but usually not more than 20 mg per day.
-Children 10 years of age and older with homozygous familial hypercholesterolemia (HoFH)—starting dose: 10 to 20 mg once a day. Doctor may increase dosage but usually not more than 80 mg per day.
